<center> # Reflector: Elevate Communication with Our Real-Time Translation Tool <big> **Explore Monadical’s Open-Source Solution for Online Meetings** </big> *Written by Hanna Jodrey and Gokul Mohanarangan. Originally published 2024-04-18 on the [Monadical blog](https://monadical.com/blog.html).* </center> ## Executive Summary In an increasingly digitized world awash with information, audio content's sheer volume and complexity pose significant challenges for analysis, accessibility, and meaningful data extraction. As a response, the Reflector platform is positioned as a potential solution designed to navigate these hurdles. Conceived as a secure, composable pipeline and hosted on an organization’s private infrastructure, its core mission is to transform speech, audio, or text into actionable knowledge while ensuring that information remains secure, private, and organized. This paper begins by highlighting the current capabilities of Reflector, showcasing its effective deployment in various settings. Following this, it discusses Reflector's inception and core motivations by providing a detailed technical overview of the current model, including its infrastructure, architecture, and the advanced AI technologies underpinning its capabilities. A critical examination of how Reflector addresses specific user needs illustrates its role in bridging communication gaps and enhancing information processing across diverse domains. Through competitive analysis, we examine Reflector's unique advantages over existing market solutions, focusing on its open-source nature, self-hosting capabilities, commitment to privacy, and ability to offer customized user experiences. Moreover, we outline potential real-world applications of Reflector, demonstrating its versatility in academic settings, facilitating communication in multilingual contexts and for individuals with speech disorders, and streamlining operations within business environments. Despite being in its initial stages, Reflector's anticipated impact and positive reception indicate a promising trajectory for revolutionizing audio content analysis. The document details an ambitious future roadmap for Reflector, outlining both short-term enhancements and long-term objectives that highlight the platform's commitment to innovation and user-centric development. In its conclusion, the post examines Reflector's promising future and invites engagement from stakeholders, potential users, and the tech community. As Reflector moves from concept to broader implementation, it stands to help redefine our engagement with digital audio content, ensuring it becomes more accessible, insightful, and actionable. ## Introduction and Background Reflector originated from a need to post-analyze presentations or idea pitches - to “reflect” on them - and determine a few essential but crucial insights. For example, could Reflector analyze whether all the planned items on the agenda were discussed and list the missed items? Could it identify which topics garnered the most or least discussion? In its initial stages, we called Reflector `agenda-talk-diff`; utilizing a simple NLP program, it employed keyword detection and worked out of a singular Python script. Despite its simplicity, `agenda-talk-diff` represented a small but significant step towards leveraging NLP to extract meaningful insights from spoken or written content. Quickly, the ambition extended beyond these basic capabilities to create a more comprehensive tool for insight generation. Reflector has advanced significantly from its humble beginnings, evolving over a few months into an increasingly robust speech-to-text (STT) translation tool. ## Current Capabilities Reflector currently boasts capabilities that enhance accessibility, comprehension, and user engagement. At its core, Reflector excels in capturing audio inputs directly from microphones or browser tabs, delivering real-time transcription services (predominantly in English, for now). Beyond transcription, it showcases a remarkable ability to simultaneously translate these transcriptions into over 100 languages, allowing users to view the original English text alongside their language of choice in real-time. Live demos have highlighted this bilingual presentation capability, marking a significant success in Reflector's deployment. Further refining the user experience, Reflector offers customizable settings to filter out potentially profane language so that the content aligns with the user's preferences and standards. To make quick sense of extended input sources and to aid in the distillation of efficient insights, Reflector intelligently breaks down audio content into live topics by providing each with a concise summary, an appropriate title, and the complete transcription. This feature empowers users to browse the topics easily to grasp the flow of the event and to examine detailed transcriptions as needed. One of Reflector's standout features is its ability to refine complex discussions into high-level summaries, capturing key ideas and takeaways in a structured, bulleted list. Coupled as it is with the generation of content-based titles, this functionality streamlines content searchability and organization significantly. Speaker diarization further enhances content clarity by identifying individual speakers throughout the recording, although this process currently unfolds in post-processing to ensure precision and accuracy in speaker differentiation. Recognizing the importance of flexibility and interaction, Reflector allows users to edit transcriptions, re-assign speakers, and modify summaries or titles. Post-recording, it leverages LLMs to field questions, drawing on content from current and past meetings based on user permissions. Sharing insights is streamlined via QR codes or automated mechanisms, such as posting to platforms like Slack and facilitating broader dissemination. Lastly, Reflector's versatility extends to pre-recorded audio, which analyzes and summarizes the content as if it were captured live. This breadth of capabilities highlights Reflector's commitment to transforming audio analysis, as it sets new methods for how we interact with and derive value from digital content. ## Technical Overview **Infrastructure** Reflector's infrastructure supports three primary deployment options that cater to its users' diverse requirements: the Monadical instance, the Media instance, and Project Reflector. Each option has been tailored to serve distinct use cases, from our internal organizational needs to broad public engagements. These options ensure that Reflector's suite of transcription, summarization, and translation services can be leveraged across various contexts. The following outlines what each deployment option offers: **Monadical Instance:** Built for Monadical’s internal use, this instance keeps our audio data secure while recording and summarizing key insights and takeaways from meetings, pitches, and presentations. **Media Instance:** Expanding Reflector's reach, the Media Instance targets external audiences in other organizations, fostering greater accessibility and insight generation. <figure> <img src="https://docs.monadical.com/uploads/c4b23631-444e-4fac-b704-8ff93d804a21.png"> <figcaption> The Reflector Media Instance. </figcaption> </img> </figure> <style> figcaption { text-align: center; /* This centers the caption text */ } </style> * **Projector Reflector:** Specifically engineered for large-scale events and conferences, this deployment option transforms audience engagement through live, on-screen displays of essential content, including critical points and translations. It showcases Reflector's ability to enhance the delivery and reception of information in public settings and ensures that content is accessible and engaging for diverse audiences. <figure> <img src="https://docs.monadical.com/uploads/26935151-89a9-4434-b41f-0c55fc08be4a.png"> <figcaption> Showcasing Project Reflector on the big screen at <a href="https://allinevent.ai/"> ALL IN </a> in 2023. </figcaption> </img> </figure> **Architecture** Reflector is powered by various advanced technologies that enhance the platform's functionality and user experience. At the forefront, Reflector's UI was developed using [React](https://react.dev/), [NextJS](https://nextjs.org/), [ChakraUI](https://chakra-ui.com/), and [Tailwind CSS](https://tailwindcss.com/), and it is designed to provide an accessible and straightforward interaction for its users. A back-end API developed with [OpenAPI](https://www.openapis.org/) anchors the platform's robust architecture. Data management relies on the combined strength of [SQLite](https://www.sqlite.org/) and [Alembic](https://alembic.sqlalchemy.org/en/latest/), which offers both security and easy accessibility of user information. Reflector employs a smart combination of APIs, Websockets, and [WebRTC](https://webrtc.org/) for real-time interactions, facilitating a direct, efficient connection to the platform’s capabilities. <figure> <img src="https://docs.monadical.com/uploads/e4a81ed8-6d33-4de6-9534-5c3be1d588cc.png"> <figcaption> Reflector’s current architecture. </figcaption> </img> </figure> <style> figcaption { text-align: center; /* This centers the caption text */ } </style> **AI Technologies** A suite of open-source AI technologies is at the heart of Reflector's functionality. Painstakingly chosen through a careful R&D process, these models are central to powering Reflector's diverse capabilities. The image below outlines some key AI models currently operational within Reflector's workflow. <figure> <img src="https://docs.monadical.com/uploads/af2ec358-704d-4736-9b83-b08283ac1f1e.png"> <figcaption> Some of the AI technologies currently used by Reflector. </figcaption> </img> </figure> <style> figcaption { text-align: center; /* This centers the caption text */ } </style> Delving deeper into core functionalities, Reflector utilizes the [Facebook seamlessM4T](https://ai.meta.com/blog/seamless-m4t/) model for translation. This model overcomes language barriers to foster and facilitate multilingual collaboration. For content analysis, the [vicuna-13b-v1.5](https://huggingface.co/lmsys/vicuna-13b-v1.5) and [Zephyr](https://huggingface.co/HuggingFaceH4/zephyr-7b-beta) models play an essential role in extracting insights from discussions. Finally, to ensure the highest quality of transcriptions, Reflector depends on the [OpenAI Whisper](https://openai.com/research/whisper) together with [Faster Whisper](https://github.com/SYSTRAN/faster-whisper) model recognized for its accuracy and clarity. ## Addressing Specific User Needs Through Features By design, Reflector's capabilities are aimed squarely at enhancing communication and comprehensive knowledge transfer, ensuring the platform is a versatile tool for various applications. To facilitate seamless communication, Reflector's architecture is built to accommodate inputs across diverse mediums—text, real-time speech, or recorded audio—delivered in multiple languages. This adaptability enables the platform to process and relay information in real-time with clarity, accuracy, and efficiency. More than auxiliary features, such as real-time transcription and translation capabilities, are central to ensuring that communication barriers, especially those posed by language differences, are effectively eliminated. Furthermore, breaking down the magnitude of input into easily digestible, “bite-sized” chunks is crucial for effective knowledge transfer. Reflector addresses this through its real-time generative features, including creating titles, topics, and summaries, significantly aiding in the comprehension and retention of knowledge. Additionally, the platform allows for a degree of customization, which permits users to tailor these outputs to suit their specific requirements better. Speaker diarization further refines the clarity of content within Reflector by distinguishing between speakers. It also enriches the context and facilitates a deeper understanding of the dialogue. Diarization and the capacity for users to edit and refine generated outputs are powerful tools for minimizing errors. It also generates a repository of user-generated ground-truth annotation, which can be used to fine-tune the models. Navigating vast datasets to locate specific pieces of information can be challenging. As a result, Reflector’s ability to chat with the data to get holistic answers to users’ queries is a notable advantage. Furthermore, the platform's design for easy sharing of these insights ensures that knowledge is readily disseminated among stakeholders, promoting collaboration and informed decision-making. Through these features, Reflector not only addresses but anticipates the needs of its users, providing an intuitive platform for managing the complexities of communication and information. <figure> <img src="https://docs.monadical.com/uploads/91161ca3-41c8-4f90-8dda-c1c0e36fed4c.png "> <figcaption> A sample session with the <a href="https://reflector.media/transcripts/new"> Reflector Media Instance </a> demonstrating live topics, speaker diarization, and meeting summary features. </figcaption> </img> </figure> ## Competitive Edge While the market is no stranger to AI-driven transcription, translation, and audio content analytics, advancements and continual improvements, many solutions remain in an extensive R&D phase without fully addressing the practical implementation required for user-facing applications. Conversely, Reflector has already developed practical implementations for real-world applications that are cost-effective, secure, and adaptable to fit individual users’ needs. Here are the unique features of Reflector that users will not find in other solutions: * **Open-source:** Monadical’s philosophy strongly emphasizes using open-source models and sharing our knowledge and expertise as we work with them. * **Self-Hosting Capability:** Reflector is currently designed to be self-hosted by end users. * Autonomy: Reflector allows end users to self-host the platform. This flexibility ensures that individuals and organizations can deploy Reflector within their own infrastructures, whether on private servers or chosen cloud providers, tailoring the environment to their specific needs. * Complete control over deployment: The self-hosted nature of Reflector grants users complete control over the platform's deployment and operation. This level of control is pivotal for users requiring custom configurations or those with stringent data governance policies. * Enhanced privacy: By enabling self-hosting, Reflector ensures that sensitive data remains within the user's control, never leaving their chosen environment. This approach is particularly crucial for data that is private, sensitive, or bound by confidentiality agreements, offering an added layer of security against unauthorized access or misuse. * **Balanced Development Approach:** Reflector is engineered to balance general applicability and customization. * Generalization: Reflector is designed to deliver insightful analytics across various meeting types— from interviews and podcasts to internal meetings—without prescribing a strict framework for information processing. * Customization for User Needs: Beyond general application, Reflector allows users to tailor the granularity, style, and data extraction format. This capability for customization ensures that the platform can adapt to varied user preferences and specific use cases. * **Focused R&D:** Reflector is committed to continuous improvement, with ongoing R&D efforts to refine core features like diarization, which enhances the clarity of transcribed content by accurately identifying and differentiating speakers. Additionally, the platform is undergoing enhancements in transcription and translation accuracy facilitated by custom post-processing functions. In addition, among the key areas of focus for future development are real-time dynamic topic detection, the integration of auto-merging topics, and the enhancement of live Q&A. ## Envisioned Real-World Applications and Impact As Reflector has been developed over the past several months, the platform's potential to redefine communicative interactions across multiple sectors has become increasingly clear. This section explores several as-of-yet-theoretical but entirely feasible scenarios that indicate how Reflector has the potential to benefit users. ### Academic Domain Reflector has the potential to enhance the student experience through: **Language inclusivity:** Through its real-time transcription and translation capabilities, Reflector aims to create a universally accessible learning environment, welcoming students from various linguistic backgrounds and contributing to a more equitable educational experience. **Efficiency in learning:** By leveraging automated note-taking and the generation of concise summaries and key points, Reflector stands to elevate study productivity and knowledge transfer. **Support for diverse learning needs:** With features that can be tailored to accommodate students facing learning obstacles, Reflector anticipates providing custom solutions that adapt to varied educational requirements; consequently, this fosters access and equity within academic settings. ### Communication Domain In the realm of communication, Reflector is anticipated to: **Facilitate multilingual engagement:** Acting as a pivotal resource for non-English speakers (such as newcomers to Canada, for instance), Reflector could play a critical role in bridging communication gaps, enabling smoother transitions and interactions within new linguistic and cultural landscapes. **Cater to varied communicative demands:** Reflector could greatly enhance communicative clarity and participation across diverse and traditionally underrepresented groups by offering essential services for individuals with speech disorders or distinct speech patterns and enabling hands-free capture of thoughts and voice memos. ### Business Environment Within the business sector, Reflector's envisioned applications include: **Meeting efficiency:** Serving as an automated moderator, Reflector has the potential to streamline corporate discussions, thus ensuring focused and effective meetings that respect participants' time and contributions. **Creation of a knowledge repository:** By transforming audio data into an organized, searchable database, Reflector could empower businesses to unlock the full value of their informational assets, enhancing strategic decision-making and insight generation. **Dynamic data interactions:** The platform's ability to facilitate interactive engagements with organizational data could revolutionize business processes by uncovering efficiencies and insights that drive superior operational performance. ## Reception and Prospective Impact In September 2023, we showcased Reflector at the ALL IN AI summit in Montreal, where its capabilities were recognized by a diverse audience of key industry players, researchers, and academics. <figure> <img src="https://docs.monadical.com/uploads/59fb16ee-5091-4b03-8a6d-f137b940a76e.jpg"> <figcaption> Monadical founder Max McCrea demonstrates Reflector at ALL IN. </figcaption> </img> </figure> <style> figcaption { text-align: center; /* This centers the caption text */ } </style> Although many of the real-world applications of Reflector remain on the horizon, the platform's foundation and initial feedback signal a bright future. Reflector is uniquely positioned to change how we engage with audio and textual information by addressing key challenges within the education, communication, and business sectors. As it continues to transition from proof of concept to reality, the anticipated range of Reflector's applications provides a window on the impact it is poised to have. ## Future Enhancements and Roadmap As Reflector progresses, a strategic blueprint has been laid out to guide its development over the coming months. This roadmap is driven by a commitment to innovation, precision, and user-centric enhancements, aiming to expand Reflector's capabilities and applications significantly. Below, we outline the ambitious goals for Reflector's near and distant future, structured within a priority matrix that evaluates the viability, delivery timeline, developer resources, and alignment with evolving business and user needs. ### Short-term Enhancements **Fine-tuned domain-specific transcription:** Develop specialized transcription models that cater to specific fields and enhance accuracy and relevance. **Speaker recognition:** Implement advanced speaker recognition technologies, utilizing fine-tuned speaker segmentation models alongside voice signatures to improve diarization. **Dynamic topic detection:** Introduce sophisticated algorithms for real-time identification and categorizing discussion topics, elevating content summarization and analysis. **Multimedia content integration:** This feature enables the upload and display of video content within Reflector, broadening the spectrum of usable media formats. **On-demand generative features:** Users can re-trigger Reflector's generative capabilities, such as artifact creation and summarization, ensuring dynamic content adaptation. **Edit history and revisions:** Establish a comprehensive revision tracking system to facilitate transparency and accountability in content modification. ### Long-term Objectives **Insight generation by meeting type:** Tailoring analytics and insight extraction based on the specific nature of meetings, offering customized information processing. **Enhanced intent extraction:** Advancements in NLP enable accurate interpretation of user intent from spoken or written inputs, improving interaction quality.Extension and integration: A browser extension will be created to simplify access to Reflector's features and integrate with platforms like Meet and Whereby for a seamless user experience. **Refinement through feedback:** Continuous optimization of LLM workflows, informed by direct user feedback to ensure alignment with user expectations. **Voice-driven NLP interface:** Introduction of a voice-activated interface, making Reflector more intuitive and accessible to a broader user base. Reflector prioritizes each enhancement based on its impact, technical feasibility, and the value it adds to the Reflector ecosystem. Decisions regarding the development timeline and allocation of resources are made collaboratively, with periodic reviews to adapt to new insights and user feedback. Notably, the roadmap recognizes the critical role of annotated data from end users in refining and advancing Reflector's capabilities, underscoring the platform's commitment to responsive and iterative development. This forward-looking plan underscores Reflector's dedication to staying at the forefront of audio content analysis technology while highlighting its responsive approach to meeting its users' changing needs. As Reflector continues to evolve, it remains firmly focused on delivering innovative, effective, and user-friendly solutions that transform how we interact with and understand audio and textual content. ## Conclusion It's clear that while Reflector is positioned to address some of the current challenges in audio content analysis, its journey is just beginning. The roadmap is filled with possibilities grounded in practical development milestones, from enhancing accessibility in education to streamlining business operations and fostering inclusive communication. Reflecting on Reflector's envisioned enhancements and the iterative approach to its evolution, we realize that the journey ahead is one of discovery, learning, and adaptation. The future enhancements we have outlined represent steps toward realizing a more connected and accessible digital environment while acknowledging the complexities and challenges that accompany technological innovation. We welcome those who see Reflector's potential and recognize its current state as a foundation upon which much can be built. Your insights, critiques, perspectives, and participation are invaluable in shaping Reflector's developmental trajectory. For updates on Reflector and opportunities to contribute to its development, we encourage you to [connect with us](https://monadical.com/contact-us.html). We welcome your thoughts and feedback and the opportunity to collaborate as we refine and test Reflector's capabilities. Together, we can approach the future of audio content analysis with a balanced optimism, mindful of the challenges ahead but motivated by the potential to create a meaningful impact in the evolving landscape of digital communication and content analysis.
